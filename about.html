Our Story

Since our launch in 2023, the team at MedMission has worked tirelessly to promote our cause. Our headquarters is located in Lutz, Florida. Our primary focus is on collecting unused and soon-to-expire medications from healthcare providers, pharmacies, and medical facilities. By repurposing these valuable resources, we ensure they reach third-world countries where they are desperately needed. This initiative not only addresses critical shortages but also prevents the wastage of essential medications. Our mission is to bridge the gap between the surplus in developed regions and the urgent needs in less privileged areas. Through our collaborative efforts with global partners and a dedicated network of volunteers, we strive to make a significant impact on health outcomes. At MedMission, we believe that everyone, regardless of their geographical location, deserves access to quality medical care. Join us in our journey to provide hope, health, and a better future for those in need.

We are three brothers from Lutz, FL, united by a profound commitment to healthcare and a deep desire to make a difference. During our travels to various countries, we observed the severe lack of medical supplies in underprivileged communities. This revelation spurred us into action.
Our mission at MedMission is to provide essential medical resources to underserved areas. We discovered that many viable medical supplies were being discarded in hospitals and clinics in our hometown, so we decided to repurpose them instead. Now, whenever we embark on our journeys, we not only donate these supplies but also establish long-term partnerships for continuous support.
With each trip, we aim to improve healthcare outcomes, enhance medical opportunities, and create a more equitable world. We believe that healthcare should have no boundaries, and together, we can save lives and improve well-being, one medical supply at a time. Join us in building a healthier future for communities in need and fostering a global network where everyone has access to the healthcare they deserve.
